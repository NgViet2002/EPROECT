Bai Xep beach

Bai Xep Beach is located in a small fishing village near the town of Quinen. Distinctive features of the beach are crystal clear water and picturesque views. The beach is surrounded by green hills, and from the sea visible boats and islands in the distance. Unusual round boats with one oar await fishermen on the coastline.

Beach description

Bai Ksep is a protected bay, so the South China Sea is calm and quiet, and the water temperature does not drop below 24 °C. Light yellow sand and clear emerald water on the deserted Bai Ksep beach create not just a feeling of an uninhabited island, but a real heavenly spot in Vietnam. The entry into the sea is quite sloping, and the shallow water is several meters long, which allows families with small children to rest comfortably.


Near the beach there is a wooden fishing boat park under the Hon Thom cableway. The cableway is the longest in the world, its length is 79 km. The route starts from the town of An Toi, adjacent to Bai Xep Beach, and runs over the sea, the islands of Hon Dua and Hon Lo and ends on the resort island of Hon Tom. From the cabins of the cableway to the beach there is a beautiful panoramic view.


When is it better to go?

Since Vietnam is a country stretching from north to south, the climate in its different parts is slightly different. In the north, high humidity and temperature are observed from May to October, but in the remaining months it is slightly drier and cooler. In the South, the rainy season is longer, lasting from may to November, but even after it ends, the humidity does not subside. Therefore, it is better to go to popular resorts in the south of the country in winter or early spring.
