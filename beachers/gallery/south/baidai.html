Bai Dai beach

Bai Dai Beach is little known among tourists. It is located about 20 km from Nha Trang, without reaching Kamran Airport. Earlier, in the 2000s, this territory was reserved for the military bases of Vietnam, and the beach was closed to the public. Bai Dai Beach on Phu Quoc Island is translated from Vietnamese as a long beach. And indeed it is: The length of the beach is about 17 km, it is located in a bay between the neighboring cities, Nha Trang and Kamran.

Beach description

The wild beach of Bai Dai is mostly deserted, most often it is visited by local Vietnamese. Bai Dai is characterized by high waves and warm water, allowing beginners to surf. In summer, the waves are too weak for professionals, but in winter they become higher. The sand on the beach is snow-white, and the water is bright turquoise with a light light-green hue. The entry into the water is shallow, especially at low tide. But despite the shallow water, it can be dangerous to swim far because of the fast undercurrents, which are warned by the posters along the beach, which prohibit swimming in certain places of Bai Dai.


Bai Dai Beach is an ideal place for exploring the wildlife of Fukuoka, although there are active building of hotel complexes near the beach, which will soon increase the flow of vacationers on the beach. Also near the beach there is a market with fresh seafood.


When is it better to go?

Since Vietnam is a country stretching from north to south, the climate in its different parts is slightly different. In the north, high humidity and temperature are observed from May to October, but in the remaining months it is slightly drier and cooler. In the South, the rainy season is longer, lasting from may to November, but even after it ends, the humidity does not subside. Therefore, it is better to go to popular resorts in the south of the country in winter or early spring.
